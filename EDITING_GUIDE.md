# Interview Video Editing Guide — Cancer Survivors Series

**Source**: Compiled from professional documentary editing workflows  
**Applies to**: Zamiyah, Vince, and all future Cancer Survivor shorts

---

## The Golden Rule

**Story first, visuals second.** Build a "radio edit" — if the story works with audio only, the visuals will follow naturally.

---

## 1. Story Construction (Before Touching the Timeline)

### Sound Bite Selection
- Read the full transcript first — don't just scan
- Mark the **money quotes** — the lines that land emotionally
- Look for **complete thoughts** — sentences that don't need setup from the interviewer
- Identify **natural in/out points** — where sentences begin and end cleanly

### The Radio Edit
- Assemble your best sound bites **audio-only** first
- Does the story flow? Does it make sense without the interviewer questions?
- If a viewer can close their eyes and follow the story, you've got it

### Narrative Arc
Every video, no matter how short, needs:
1. **Hook** — grab them in the first 5 seconds
2. **Setup** — who is this person?
3. **Tension** — what happened / what's at stake?
4. **Emotional core** — the moment that hits hardest
5. **Resolution** — growth, hope, takeaway
6. **Close** — tagline, outro

---

## 2. Cut Timing & Pacing

### Interview Cut Rules
- **Cut on the first word** — not after a delay
- **If someone is talking, show them** — even 1s interjections
- **Minimum speaking hold**: 1 second
- **Minimum hold before cutting to reaction**: 2 seconds into the speaker's line
- **Don't cut for filler** — "mm-hmm" or "yeah" as agreement, stay on speaker

### Pacing
- **Average cut rate**: 1 cut every 8-15 seconds for conversational content
- **Vary the rhythm** — don't cut at the same interval every time
- **Let stories breathe** — don't cut away just because it's been 30 seconds
- **Quick-cut for energy**, longer holds for emotional weight

### B-Roll Timing
- **Sync to key phrases** — introduce B-roll as the speaker mentions relevant topics
- **Don't overuse** — words remain the focus, B-roll supports, doesn't replace
- **3-5 seconds per B-roll insert** is the sweet spot
- **Match pacing** — relaxed tone = longer B-roll holds, energetic = quick cuts

---

## 3. Camera Switching (Multicam Interview)

| Scenario | Camera |
|:---------|:-------|
| Subject speaking (default) | **Tight/Close-up** |
| Topic transitions | **Wide** (visual reset) |
| Opening/closing | **Wide** (establish scene) |
| Long pauses / crosstalk | **Wide** |
| Brief reactions (1-2s) | Opposite camera |
| Emotional moments | **Hold on subject, do NOT cut** |

### Critical: Don't Cut During Emotional Peaks
When a subject hits their key moment — tears, revelation, power statement — **stay on them**. The camera holds. The audience connects. Cutting away during these moments kills the impact.

---

## 4. Hiding Edits & Jump Cuts

When you need to cut out ums, pauses, or restarts:
- **B-roll cutaway** — the go-to technique, covers the edit completely
- **Camera angle switch** — in multicam, switch to the other angle
- **L-cut / J-cut** — audio leads or trails the video cut for smoother transitions
- **Crossfade** — subtle audio crossfade (100-200ms) smooths dialogue edits
- **Zoom punch-in** — reframe the same shot slightly tighter to mask the cut (use sparingly)

---

## 5. Audio Hierarchy

1. **Master audio** (TASCAM/external recorder) — always priority
2. **Camera scratch audio** — reference only, for sync verification
3. **Music bed** — low and under, never competing with dialogue
4. **Natural ambience** — room tone to fill gaps between edits

### Audio Levels
- **Dialogue**: -12dB to -6dB (peaks)
- **Music bed (under dialogue)**: -24dB to -18dB
- **Music bed (no dialogue)**: -12dB to -8dB
- **Always leave 0.5s of room tone** before and after each sound bite for breathing room

## 6. Structure for This Series

Based on the six existing CAC videos (~2:00-2:30 each):

```
[0:00-0:05]  HOOK — strongest single line, cold open
[0:05-0:15]  IDENTITY — "My name is...", who they are
[0:15-0:45]  SETUP — their world, their personality, their connection
[0:45-1:15]  THE STORY — the cancer connection, what happened
[1:15-1:45]  EMOTIONAL CORE — the line that hits hardest
[1:45-2:10]  RESOLUTION — growth, advice, empowerment
[2:10-2:20]  TAGLINE — "You're worth it"
[2:20-2:40]  OUTRO — Lashawnda VO + end cards
```

### The Cold Open (Hook)
Don't start with "Hi, my name is..." — **start with the most compelling line**, then cut to the identity. This is the YouTube retention technique: grab them first, introduce second.

---

## 7. Common Mistakes

- ❌ Starting with the identity intro (boring, no hook)
- ❌ Cutting too late (showing listener when speaker starts talking)
- ❌ Never showing reactions (feels like a slideshow)
- ❌ Cutting mid-sentence when same person is still talking
- ❌ Using wide shot too much (interview = intimacy = close-ups)
- ❌ Cutting away during emotional peaks
- ❌ Making every cut the same length (robotic pacing)
- ❌ Excessive B-roll that overshadows the subject's words
- ❌ Not leaving room tone between edits (choppy audio)
